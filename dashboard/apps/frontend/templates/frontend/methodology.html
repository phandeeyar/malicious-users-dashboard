Methodology

The results of the analysis are based on network modelling: First, according to a lexicon of common hate speech expressions in Burmese compiled by Phandeeyar, we labelled hate speech comments made by users on popular pages and groups in Myanmar. We then calculated the total number of hate speech comments a user made, the overall number of comments a user made and the ratio of non-hate speech comments to hate speech comments. Secondly, for each week, a network showing the interaction between users commenting on posts and replying to each other’s comments on these pages and groups was then created. We say that two users are connected if one of them has replied to the other person’s comment. Based on the connections shown in the network graph, we calculated the degree centrality and the PageRank of each user to see their influence in the network. Degree centrality is the defined as the number of connections that a user has. A more influential users that has a lot of other users replying to his comments will score higher in the metric than a user whose comments do not get a lot of attention from other users. The PageRank measure counts the number and quality of connections a user has and estimates the importance of that user in the network. Based on these two measures, we found the most central users in the network. The overall malicious score is a compound measure of all the measures calculated.
